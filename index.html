The approach

The work is with your nervous system, not your willpower.

Most stress isn't a thinking problem. It's a body that learned to stay on high alert and never quite stood down. You can't reason your way out of that - but you can teach your system that it's safe to soften. That's what we practise together, step by step.

Three brains, one in charge

Survival, emotional, thinking. Under stress the survival brain grabs the wheel and locks you in fight, flight or freeze. We start by noticing who's driving.

From overdrive to rest

Your autonomic nervous system has two gears. We learn to move out of sympathetic overdrive and into the parasympathetic state where you rest, digest and repair.

Naming the responses

Fight, flight, freeze, fawn, flop - each is your body trying to keep you safe. Once you can recognise yours, you can meet it with a way to settle instead of fighting it.

A practice you keep

Self-hypnosis is a daily self-regulation tool: keyword anchoring, the laws of repetition and association, and a simple 21-day cycle that makes calm a habit rather than a hope.

Two ways to begin

Learn it in a group, or work one to one.

Both lead to the same place - a calmer, more regulated you. Choose the pace and the company that fit your life right now.
